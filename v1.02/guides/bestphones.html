The Best Smartphones to Buy in 2021

This list has been carefully curated with maximum performance per price in mind.
Prices and availability may differ between regions.

If you've found something not mentioned here...

  • It probably isn't worth buying because it's either too old, something better can be had for the same price or you're spending too much for minimal improvement over another option.


🗔 Mobile Operating Systems (OSs)

💚 Android

  • Free, open-source software.
  • Allows for easy customisation and installation of third-party apps outside of the Google Play Store.
  • May come with various skins (One UI, MiUI) and spyware from manufacturers that can make using the device less comfortable.
  • Stock ROMs can be replaced with custom ROMs, e.g. LineageOS, GrapheneOS or CalyxOS, which are more privacy and security orientated.
  • Now supports FaceTime calls.
  • Can feel a little stuttery at times.

🍎 Apple iOS

  • Closed-source.
  • Very well optimised for Apple hardware and as a result, feels very smooth despite Apple using low refresh rate screens.
  • Restricted customisability.
  • Native support for proprietary communication platforms (iMessage and FaceTime).
  • Installation of third-party apps external to the App Store is impossible without using AltStore (restricted to 3 apps) or jailbreaking.
  • Pings Apple hundreds of times a minute and includes on-device scanning from iOS 15 onwards.
