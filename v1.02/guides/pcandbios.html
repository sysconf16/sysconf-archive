  • Cooling unit for the processor (CPU).
  • Come as air coolers or water coolers.

  • Substance which conducts heat from the CPU to the CPU cooler.
  • Sits between the cold plate of the CPU cooler and the integrated heat spread (IHS) of the CPU.

âš™ï¸ Memory (RAM)

  • Volatile, working memory which contains active process and application data.

ğŸ”³ Motherboards

  • Backbone of the computer, allowing for communication between all components.
  • Must support the processor and memory of choice.
  • Found in a variety of sizes (micro-ATX, ATX, extended-ATX, ITX).
  • Found with a variety of sockets depending on CPU support (AM4, LGA 1200).

ğŸ”§ The Build

0ï¸âƒ£ Prerequisites

  1. Collect your parts and lay them on a large surface, e.g. a table.
  2. Ground yourself by touching a large, metal object around your house, e.g. a radiator.

1ï¸âƒ£ Motherboard Preperation

  1. Open the motherboard box and remove the motherboard from its anti-static bag.
  2. Close the motherboard box and place the motherboard on top.
  3. Open the CPU box and remove the CPU tray.
  4. Open the CPU socket by lifting the lever up and to the opposite side until you hear a click if AMD, or pull the latch down and out then lift the lid upwards if Intel.
  5. Open the CPU tray and remove the CPU, holding it by the edges to not touch the pads or pins on the underside.
  6. Insert the CPU into the socket by aligning the arrow on the socket with the arrow printed on the CPU.
  7. Close the socket in the reverse way you opened it. There may be some resistance if installing an Intel CPU.
  8. Remove the RAM from its packaging.
  9. Refer to the motherboard's manual to choose the correct slots to insert the DIMMs into for dual channel memory mode if two DIMMS are being used.
  10. Open the latches on the sides of the DIMM slots on the motherboard.
  11. Align the notch in the centre of the DIMM with the notch in the centre of the slot in the motherboard.
  12. Insert each stick one by one, pushing firmly on the edges of the stick to seat it within the slot.
  13. If installing an M.2 drive, remove it from the packaging.
  14. Refer to the motherboard's manual to choose the correct M.2 slot to install the drive to ensure correct compatibility, e.g. if a PCIe Gen 4 drive is being used, ensure that the drive is being installed into a PCIe Gen 4 compliant M.2 slot.
  15. Unscrew the M.2 screw from the stand-off or if not pre-installed, find the M.2 screw in the motherboard box.
  16. Slide the pins of the M.2 drive into the slot with the label facing outwards.
  17. If the screw hole of the M.2 drive does not align with the stand-off, remove the drive and move the stand-off to a different hole on the board.
  18. Screw the M.2 drive into the slot.
  19. If using a stock or aftermarket air cooler, remove the cooler from its packaging.
  20. If using a water cooler, leave it in the packaging until later.
  21. Follow the instructions provided with the cooler to ensure proper installation. Each cooler will differ in installation technique.
  22. If applying thermal paste, remove the syringe cap and push a pea sized blob out onto the centre of the CPU.
  23. The motherboard should now be prepared with CPU, RAM, SSD and cooler installed.

2ï¸âƒ£ï¸ Case Preperation

  1. Remove the case from its packaging.
  2. Unscrew and remove both side-panels by unscrewing the thumb-screws on the front or rear of the side-panel depending on case model.
  3. Remove the front-panel from the case if additional fans are going to be installed.
  4. Remove the accessory box from the case.
  5. Remove any additional fans from their packaging and screw them into the case using the included self-tapping screws.
  6. Lay the case on its side.
  7. If the motherboard does not have a pre-installed I/O shield, re-open the motherboard box and remove the I/O shield.
  8. Remove the I/O shield from its packet and install it in the correct orientation at the rear of the case.
  9. The case is now ready for the motherboard to be mounted.

3ï¸âƒ£ï¸ Motherboard Mounting

  1. Test-fit the motherboard into the case to ensure that all of the pre-installed motherboard stand-offs in the case align with the screw holes on the motherboard.
  2. If the test-fit is not successful, use pliers to remove the misplaced stand-offs and place them into the correct locations on the motherboard tray.
  3. Re-fit the motherboard into the case ensuring that the rear I/O aligns with the I/O shield.
  4. Open the case's accessories box and remove the screw packet
  5. Open the screw packet and pour the screws onto a bowl or plate
  6. Use the curved circular screws to screw the motherboard into the motherboard tray.
  7. If using a water cooler, remove it from its packaging.
  8. Mount the radiator to the case. It is recommended to install this at the top if possible. Gamers Nexus
  9. Follow the instructions provided with the cooler to ensure proper installation of the waterblock. Each cooler will differ in installation technique.
  10. The motherboard is now mounted in the case.

4ï¸âƒ£ Drives, Expansion Cards & Power Supply

  1. Refer to the motherboard manual's front-panel connector layout and install the front-panel connectors (power switch, reset switch, power LED, disk activity LED, front-panel audio and USB) to the motherboard.
  2. Open and mount any 2.5" or 3.5" drives to the drive bays using the hexagonal-shaped headed screws from the case accessory box.
  3. If a 2.5" or 3.5" drive has been mounted, remove a SATA cable from the motherboard box and use it to connect the drive to the motherboard's SATA ports.
  4. Remove the PSU from its packaging.
  5. Insert the PSU into the case and mount it, often at the rear, using the screws included.
  6. Connect the 24-pin motherboard power cable from the PSU to the right side of the motherboard.
  7. Connect the 8-pin CPU EPS power cable from the PSU to the top left of the motherboard.
  8. If your motherboard only has a 4-pin EPS power connector, split the 8-pin from the PSU into two halves and insert the half which fits.
  9. Connect the SATA power cables from the PSU to any installed 2.5" or 3.5" drives.
  10. Remove any brackets covering the PCI connectors on the rear of the case.
  11. If installing a discrete graphics card, remove it from its packaging.
  12. If the card is single slot, remove the PCI blanking plate corresponding to the 16X slot from the rear of the case.
  13. If the card is 2+ slots, remove the PCI blanking plates corresponding to the 16X slot and additional underneath from the rear of the case.
  14. Remove any platic covers over the PCI-E 16X connector on the graphics card.
  15. Push the GPU into the motherboard's PCI-E 16X slot.
  16. Screw the graphics card into the case using the hexagonal-shaped headed screws from the case accessory box.
  17. Connect any PCI-E peripheral power connectors from the PSU to the GPU if required.
  18. If installing a Wi-Fi or Bluetooth card, remove it from its packaging.
  19. Remove the PCI blanking plate corresponding to the PCI-E slot into which the card is to be installed on the motherboard.
  20. Insert the card into the PCI-E slot of the motherboard.
  21. Screw the Wi-Fi or Bluetooth card into the case using the hexagonal-shaped headed screws from the case accessory box.

5ï¸âƒ£ Finishing Touches

  1. Re-install any brackets covering the PCI slots on the rear of the case.
  2. Using zip ties or velcro straps, tie down as many cables as possible at the rear of the motherboard tray.
  3. Re-install the front and side-panels in the reverse way by which they were removed.
  4. Connect a mouse, keyboard and monitor to the computer, ensuring to plug the monitor into the graphics card and not the motherboard if a discrete GPU is being used.
  5. Remove the kettle lead from the PSU box and plug the motherboard into the mains.
  6. Flick the switch on the back of the PSU to the on position and press the power button.
  7. Wait for the PC to POST, while repeatedly pressing the BIOS key on the keyboard to enter the BIOS. This may take up to a minute on first boot. See below for the BIOS key of each motherboard manufacturer.
  8. Once in the BIOS, verify that the CPU, RAM, and drives are correctly detected.
  9. Shut down the PC by holding the power button.
  10. The PC is now ready for the BIOS to be configured.

ğŸ“¤ Updating the BIOS

  1. Using another computer, download the latest BIOS revision for your motherboard from the manufacturers website.
  2. Format a USB stick with the FAT32 file system.
  3. Extract the BIOS image from the downloaded archive.
  4. Copy the BIOS image to the root of the USB stick, e.g. if the drive letter is "D", D:\.
  5. Insert the USB stick into one of the rear USB 2.0 ports on the motherboard.
  6. Press the power button and enter the BIOS by repeatedly pressing the BIOS key.
  7. Enter the BIOS flash/update utility for your motherboard.
  8. Select the file from the USB stick and wait for the BIOS to update.
  9. The system may power cycle multiple times. This is normal.
  10. The newest BIOS revision is now installed.

âš™ï¸ Making Changes in the BIOS (for Windows 11 Compatibility)

  1. Press the power button and enter the BIOS by repeatedly pressing the BIOS key.
  2. Enable XMP.
  3. Enable UEFI boot and disable legacy boot.
  4. Disable PXE/network/LAN boot.
  5. Enable TPM by looking for "Intel PTT" on Intel chipset motherboards or "AMD fTPM" on AMD chipset motherboards.
  6. If there's an option to specify OS type, select "Windows 10 WHQL".
  7. Disable virtualisation, unless you know you'll be running virtual machines, by disabling "Intel Virtualisation Tech (VT-x)" on Intel chipset motherboards and "SVM" on AMD chipset motherboards.
