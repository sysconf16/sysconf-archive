ğŸ“ Notes for Processors (CPU) w/ Integrated Graphics (iGPU)

Intel CPUs with iGPUs are NOT recommended because:

  • Marginal CPU performance benefit over budget pre-built machines with identical iGPU performance at a much greater price premium.
  • Many budget H510 and B560 chipset motherboards lack HDMI 2.0 or DisplayPort 1.2 for 4K @ 60Hz output, something which can be readily found on budget pre-builts at a fraction of the price of a complete custom Intel iGPU system.

ğŸ“ Notes for Processors (CPU) w/o Integrated Graphics (iGPU)

AMD Ryzen 9 5950X is NOT recommended because:

  • Once all 16C/32T eventually become commonly utilised in applications, CPU architecture will have improved enough so that single-core performance of a 5950X will be awful compared to new CPU releases.
  • This will limit usefulness and an upgrade will be advised.
  • It is recomended to purchase a lower-tier SKU and save for future upgrades rather than attempting to "future-proof".

ğŸ“ Notes for CPU (Air & Water) Coolers

Using the stock cooler is recommended for budget tier CPU options because:

  • Low TDP and therefore low heat output.
  • A cooler at this tier will unnecessarily increase the costs of the build.

ğŸ“ Notes for Motherboards for AMD CPUs (AM4)

X570 chipset motherboards have NOT been considered due to:

  • Minimal improvement over top B550 boards while being considerably more expensive.
  • Not necessary other than for workstations that require many PCI-E lanes.
  • Loud, whiney chipset fans.

ğŸ“ Notes for Motherboards for Intel CPUs (LGA 1200)

B560 chipset motherboards are NOT recommended because of:

  • Poor performance with higher tier CPUs due to power limiting and throttling.

Z490 chipset motherboards are NOT recommended because of:

  • Too similar pricing to Z590 motherboards despite offering worse VRM setups.
  • May be worth considering if a board can be found considerably cheaper than the Z590 options.

ğŸ“ Notes for Storage

Hard drives (HDDs) are NOT recommended because:

  • Extremely slow performance in comparison to solid state drives (SSDs).

Kingston drives are NOT recommended because:

  • Very slow, unconsistent performance compared to the rest of the competition.

Avoid Samsung QVO drives because:

  • Worse quality NAND flash compared to other drives at a similar price, unless they can be bought for a VERY cheap price.

ğŸ“ Notes for Graphics Cards (GPUs)

Nvidia GeForce GTX 1660 Supers are NO LONGER recommended because:

  • Cost similarly to RTX 2060s.
  • Do not support DLSS.

Nvidia GeForce RTX 2070s are NO LONGER recommended because:

  • Can be found at the same price as SUPER models, which offer superior performance.
  • May be worth considering if can be found significantly cheaper than a SUPER model.

Nvidia GeForce RTX 3080Tis and 3090s are NOT recommended because:

  • Minimal performance gains over a 3080.
  • Additional VRAM not useful for majority of use cases.

Upper tier 30-series models NOT recommended because:

  • Ampere overclocks poorly making them bad value for money.

Corsair TX650M is NOT recommended because:

  • Too similarly priced to the 750W version making it poor value for money.

Corsair RM(non-x) series is NOT recommended because:

  • Inferior capacitor selection compared to the RMx series.

80+ Gold compliant Corsair SF series is NOT recommended because:

  • Too similarly priced to the 80+ Platinum compliant models making them poor value for money.
  • Worth considering if they can be found considerably cheaper than the platinum models.

ğŸ“ Notes for Pre-Built PCs

Ultra-small-form-factor (USFF) and desktop machines are NOT recommended because:

  • Limited upgradability due to size and entirely proprietary hardware.

T-series Intel CPUs machines are NOT recommended because:

  • Too similarly priced to non-T-series machines but with worse single-core and multi-core performance due to lower clockspeeds.

7th and 6th generation Intel and older machines are NOT recommended because:

  • Are NOT readily Windows 11 compatible.
  • Too similarly priced to 8th generation machines but with worse multi-core performance due to less CPU cores.

4th generation Intel and older machines are NOT recommended because:

  • Are NOT readily Windows 11 compatible.
  • Offer half-ish of the performance of an 8th generation machine despite being over half of the cost.
  • Poor iGPU performance, lacking the ability to decode some modern media formats.
  • Security vulnerabilities have crippled performance severely.

Mid tier and above pre-built machines from OEM manufacturers (Dell, HP, Alienware, Lenovo, etc) are NOT recommended because of:

  • Low quality, proprietary hardware selection which reduce performance and longevity compared to a custom built with the equivalent cost.
