The Best Laptops to Buy in 2021

This list has been carefully curated with maximum performance per price in mind.
Prices and availability may differ between regions.

If you've found something not mentioned here...

  • It probably isn't worth buying because it's either too old, something better can be had for the same price or you're spending too much for minimal improvement over another option.


💻 Laptop Classes

📏 Thin & Lights

  • Great for most individuals and a variety of use cases, including school, office work and general internet browsing or email.
  • Prioritise portability with a small form factor and long battery life.
  • May lack I/O options and expandibility.
  • Variable build quality depending on model and manufacturer.

💼 Business

  • Great for most individuals and a variety of use cases, including school, office work and general internet browsing or email.
  • Prioritise connectivity, often with ample I/O options and expandibility.
  • Known for superior build quality and extended longevity.
  • May lack premium features on lower-end tiers, e.g. small form factor, high resolution or colour accurate screens and long battery life.

🎮 Gaming

  • Useful for those requiring better graphical capabilities than integrated graphics options can offer, e.g. for gaming, 3D modelling, design or video editing and effects.
  • Prioritise connectivity, often with ample I/O options and expandibility.
  • Known for being large and heavy with sub-par battery life.
  • Run hotter and slower than a desktop with equivalent specifications.

In cases where better graphical performance is required, a desktop PC is highly recommended due to better price/performance.
Please see the desktop PC hardware options here.



📏 💼 Thin & Lights or Business

💲 Budget Tier

£200 - £350 | Intel Core i5 8250U/8350U/8265U machines

  • Good CPU performance for the price. PassMark for an 8250U.
  • Undervolt very well for additional performance and battery life.
  • Will need to be found used on eBay. Condition and battery health may therefore vary.
  • Finding a machine with a minimum of 8GB RAM, an SSD and a 1080p resolution screen is recommended as expandibility on consumer devices may be limited. May be less of a problem on business class machines.

£300 - £350 | AMD Ryzen 3 4300U machines

  • Superior single-core and multi-core performance in comparison to an 8250U due to architectural improvements in Zen 2 versus Kaby Lake Refresh. PassMark.
  • Scarce availability due to limited production.
  • Can be found used on websites such as eBay for a reduced price. Condition and battery health may vary however.
  • Most devices lack expandibility and can only be found with 4GB of soldered RAM.
  • Should only be considered if found with a minimum of 8GB RAM, an SSD and a 1080p resolution screen, unless upgradibility is confirmed. May be less of a problem on business class machines.

💲💲 Mid Tier

£450 - £550 | AMD Ryzen 5 4600u or 5500U machines

  • Great CPU and GPU performance for the price. PassMark.
  • Most devices will come with 8GB of soldered RAM. If extra RAM is necessary, find a device with 16GB RAM or verify upgradability before purchase.

£550 - £700 | AMD Ryzen 7 4800u or 5700U machines

  • Better performance than a 4800u for a similar/equivalent price. PassMark.
  • Other details similar to above.

💲💲💲 High Tier

£800 - £1250 | Apple MacBook Air 2020 with M1 Chip

  • Best performing laptop to date with stellar battery life.
  • Passive cooling only so not suited for prolonged intensive workloads.
  • ARM SOC which cannot run x86 operating systems or software. Restricted to proprietary macOS.
  • Non-upgradable hardware. The 8GB RAM and 256GB SSD base model may not be suitable for some.
  • Privacy concerns with on-device scanning from macOS 12 Monetery onwards.
  • Can be purchased from theEDUstore, with a .edu email address for £830.48.


🎮 Gaming

💲 Budget Tier

  • Not recommended at this price tier due to poor performance and lack of upgradability.
  • Please see the budget gaming desktop PC hardware options here instead.

💲💲 Mid Tier

£550 - £650 | AMD Ryzen 5 4600H machines

  • Superior multi-core performance in comparison to an i5 10300H machine, but slightly worse single-core performance. PassMark.
  • Usually paired with a GTX 1650 or 1650Ti and 8GB RAM, which I recommend upgrading to a dual-channel kit of 16GB 3200MHz RAM for better performance (~£50).*
  • One option is the HP Pavilion 15-ec1008na, which can be found here for £599.97.

£550 - £650 | Intel Core i5 10300H machines

  • Inferior multi-core performance in comparison to an i5 10300H machine, but slightly better single-core performance. PassMark.
  • Often paired with a GTX 1650/1650Ti or higher and 8GB RAM, which I recommend upgrading to a dual-channel kit of 16GB RAM for better performance (~£50).

💲💲💲 High Tier

£1000 - £1250 | AMD Ryzen 5 5800H machines

  • Excellent CPU performance for an x86-based machine. PassMark.
  • Often paired with an RTX 3060.
  • Avoid 8GB RAM models.


📝 Notes

Intel Core iX 10th and 11th Generation U-Series based machines are NOT recommended because:

  • Worse single-core and multi-core performance in comparison to their AMD counterparts.
  • Cannot be undervolted due to Plundervolt vulnerability patches after Q2 2020. This makes battery life and thermals worse than their AMD counterparts.

AMD Ryzen 5 5600H based machines have not been included because of:

  • Similar pricing to 5800H based machines.
  • Inferior performance in comparison to a 5800H.

*16GB and 32GB DDR4 3200MHz SO-DIMM memory kits are largely unavailable new from retailers, but new or slightly used kits can often be found on eBay for auction.
